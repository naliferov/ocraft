ocraft as a product — monetization, channels & go-to-market plan
================================================================
(research + plan written 2026-06-23; researched, not vibes — sources at the end)

GOAL (as stated): convert ocraft into a PRODUCT and make it succeed across
channels — as a BUSINESS *and* as an OPEN-SOURCE project on GitHub.

This plan is the business/GTM companion to project-direction-and-ideas.txt (which
is about what to BUILD). It applies the README "Monetization & marketing lens" at
the whole-project level. Four research streams fed it: GitHub Sponsors economics,
donation/membership platforms, how comparable projects fund themselves, and the
build-in-public audience->revenue funnel. Verification tags below: [V] verified
from a primary/official source, [E] estimate / single-source / aged.


0. TL;DR — THE ONE-PAGE STRATEGY
--------------------------------

  - DON'T take VC. A free, self-hostable, niche dev tool cannot produce
    venture-scale returns; VC + OSS + solo burnout = the Dendron death spiral.
    The proven template for THIS product shape is Obsidian / Excalidraw /
    Capacities: bootstrap, stay local-first, monetize a thin optional layer. [V]

  - The binding constraint is GEOGRAPHY, not pricing. A Ukraine-resident
    individual CANNOT receive GitHub Sponsors, Ko-fi, Liberapay, Buy-Me-a-Coffee
    or Polar payouts — they all ride Stripe Connect / PayPal-receive, neither of
    which operates in Ukraine. [V] Decide the receiving vehicle FIRST (section 1),
    or every "add a donate button" task is dead on arrival.

  - Audience precedes money. Median GitHub Sponsors income is ~$0; >60% of
    Sponsors profiles earn nothing; stars do NOT convert passively. [V] The real
    asset is an off-GitHub audience (X followers + an email list). Build-in-public's
    actual job is growing that list. Ship the audience before the donate button.

  - ocraft's unfair advantage is that it's VISUAL. p5/Three scenes, generative
    art, "drag a file -> it's a node", "a note that IS a program" — every one is a
    <30s demo clip. That is exactly the novelty that grows a dev-tool audience on
    X and wins a Show HN. Lead with it.

  - Sequence: build audience (X clips + devlog) 60-90 days -> Show HN / Product
    Hunt launch -> monetize with a digital product built ON ocraft (scene/template
    packs), GitHub-Sponsors-equivalent live the whole time via a Ukraine-viable
    rail. Open-core SaaS is parked (highest effort, multi-year ops, MIT is the most
    clonable license).


1. THE HARD CONSTRAINT FIRST — UKRAINE PAYOUT REALITY  [decide this before anything]
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Root cause: Stripe does not operate in Ukraine (NBU confirmed Nov 2025 that Stripe
"never even applied for a license"), and PayPal in Ukraine is SEND-ONLY — it cannot
receive/withdraw business funds. [V] The working rails into Ukraine are PAYONEER and
WISE (receive USD/EUR -> cash out to a Ukrainian IBAN; never request UAH). [V]

Platform-by-platform, for a Ukraine RESIDENT with no foreign entity:

  USABLE TODAY (zero foreign setup):
    - Patreon ............ YES, via Payoneer. Patreon's own help page states UA
                          creators have no PayPal/Hyperwallet option; Payoneer is
                          the route. ~10% platform + processing + ~2% Payoneer. [V]
    - Open Collective .... YES, via Wise (USD/EUR). Fiscal host (Open Source
                          Collective) holds funds, you file expenses, paid via
                          Wise. 10% host fee. Repo must live under a GitHub ORG. [V]

  NOT USABLE from Ukraine without a foreign entity (all Stripe/PayPal-receive):
    - GitHub Sponsors .... NO. Ukraine is not in GitHub's ~104 supported regions
                          (Poland, Romania, Moldova ARE — Ukraine is the gap),
                          because Sponsors payouts ride Stripe Connect. [V]
    - Ko-fi .............. NO (Stripe/PayPal payout only). [V]
    - Liberapay ......... NO (pays into your own Stripe/PayPal). [V]
    - Buy Me a Coffee ... NO / not reliable (UA not on supported list; the "Wise"
                          path is a one-off 2024 support exception, not documented). [V]
    - Polar ............. NO (Stripe Connect Express; UA absent from supported list). [V]

DECISION (the one thing to choose now):

  (a) STAY a Ukraine individual ->  Patreon (Payoneer) is the day-one recurring
      rail; Open Collective (Wise) is the project-funding rail. Put THESE in
      .github/FUNDING.yml — never a bare github: button you can't get paid through.

  (b) INCORPORATE ABROAD (Stripe Atlas US LLC, or an EE/PL/UK Ltd) ->  unlocks
      Stripe direct + Polar + Lemon Squeezy + Ko-fi simultaneously. This is real
      business/tax overhead, but it is ALSO the thing you'd need anyway to sell
      digital products cleanly (MoR VAT) or ever run a hosted SaaS. Do it when
      there's a real product to sell, not before.

  CAVEAT TO CONFIRM (load-bearing, not officially documented): that Open Source
  Collective will actually pay a Ukraine resident via Wise. Plausible from the
  docs, but verify with OSC directly before building on it. Confirm Ukraine tax
  treatment (FOP Group 3 ~5%, Payoneer ~2%) with a Ukrainian accountant. [E]


2. WHAT "OCRAFT AS A PRODUCT" ACTUALLY IS  (the open core vs. the paid layer)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

ocraft today is single-user, localhost, local-first, MIT. That is a FEATURE for
the open-source side and a CONSTRAINT for the business side. Resolve it with a
ladder — keep the substrate free and open (it's the marketing + the credibility),
sell thin artifacts and conveniences ON TOP:

  FREE / OPEN (MIT, on GitHub — this is the funnel, not the product):
    - The node OS core: the store + API, the Vue editor, html/script/scene nodes,
      the x context. Stars, forks, Show HN, "I built a node OS" devlogs all run on
      this being open and runnable.

  PAID LAYER (what people actually buy — lowest audience needed first):
    1. DIGITAL PACKS built on the free core — premium scene2d/scene3d templates,
       generative-art recipe packs, pixel-art SVG sprite sets, node "starter kits"
       (a pre-wired project tree). This is the Tailwind -> Tailwind UI pattern:
       give the engine away, sell the premium content made with it. Maps directly
       to existing roadmap items: pixel-art-svg, generative-art, binary node
       (the format media packs ship as). [V pattern: Tailwind UI ~$2M/5mo]
    2. COMMERCIAL LICENSE, Obsidian-style — free for individuals, ~$50/user/yr for
       company use. Zero infrastructure to enforce; monetizes businesses without
       gating hobbyists. [V model]
    3. SDK / "remove-the-watermark" LICENSE (tldraw model) — only if the scene/
       script-node engine becomes embeddable in others' products: free with a
       "made with ocraft" mark, paid key to remove it for commercial use. Highest
       leverage IF others build on the engine; not day one. [V model]
    4. HOSTED "pro" version (open-core SaaS) — PARKED. Highest effort (multi-year
       ops, on-call, support), and pure-MIT is the most clonable license, so a
       hosted ocraft is the easiest thing for someone else to also host. If ever
       pursued, gate the paid modules under AGPL/source-available (Plausible
       tightened its license for exactly this reason), and note it needs the
       keystone (handlers-as-nodes) + sandbox shipped first to be multi-tenant. [V]

  Honest tension to hold: README "Direction" frames ocraft as a PERSONAL
  life-management / learning / portfolio vehicle. The product path doesn't replace
  that — the personal dogfooding + build-in-public IS the marketing engine, and the
  paid packs are a byproduct of building the thing for yourself in public.


3. THE BUSINESS MODEL — WHAT THE COMPARABLES PROVE
--------------------------------------------------

WORKS for a local-first, solo, niche tool:
  - Bootstrapped local-first core + thin paid add-on. Obsidian (~$25M ARR [E,
    reported], 7 people, NO VC), Excalidraw (bootstrapped by choice, Excalidraw+
    SaaS), Capacities. Local-first = near-zero server cost = a solo dev can be
    profitable. [V model; Obsidian $ figure unverified by Obsidian]
  - Commercial license atop a capable free tier. TouchDesigner (free capped ->
    $300/$600/$2,200), Obsidian's $50/yr commercial. Charge those who make money
    with it. [V]
  - SDK / watermark license. tldraw ($2.7M+$2M+$10M raised; ~$6k/yr commercial
    license [E reported]). [V model]

KILLS projects like this:
  - OSS + VC mismatch -> Dendron: local-first markdown PKM, ~100k users, raised
    $2M seed, founder's own words "not able to find product market fit for a
    venture-backed business" + burnout -> wind-down, relicensed Apache 2.0, NO
    community fork emerged because he pivoted the company instead of handing off
    maintainership. The archetypal trap. AVOID. [V]
  - Donations as a salary plan. Open Collective at this tier yields ~$1.5k-$258k/yr
    (Hydra ~$1.5k, TidalCycles ~£5.5k, even Logseq ~$258k with an $4.1M seed) —
    subsidizes a hobby or one frugal dev, never a team. Supplement, not income. [V]
  - Taking the seed but stalling the ship (Logseq, Roam losing users). Seed-less +
    consistent shipping beats funded + stalled.

The creative-coding nonprofit/endowment route (Processing Foundation: a one-off
~$10.9M crypto-donation year -> $8.7M endowment) is not realistically reachable for
a solo project; don't plan around a windfall. [V]


4. AUDIENCE -> REVENUE FUNNEL + CHANNELS  (build-in-public)
----------------------------------------------------------

THE FUNNEL:  content (demo clips, devlogs) -> followers -> EMAIL LIST -> product/sponsors
Benchmark leak rates (industry aggregates unless named):
  - follower -> email subscriber: 2-5% healthy. Real: Daniel Vassallo 24k -> 880
    (~3.7%). [V]
  - email -> sale: 2-5% on a good list; one-off broadcasts far lower (~0.16%). Real:
    Vassallo's 880-sub list produced $7,552 of a $32.5k launch (~$8.58/sub). [V]
  - Sponsorship/product floor: ~500-5,000 ENGAGED followers is enough to launch;
    "NontechAna" sold 41 x $19 templates ($898) to ~1,019 followers (~4%). [V]
  - Dev/tech newsletter CPM is among the highest ($60-150). [E/AGG]

CHANNELS (make ONE Screen-Studio clip per feature, distribute everywhere):

  X / Twitter  [PRIMARY — ocraft's biggest advantage]
    Format: native <30s demo clip (not a link), hook in 1-2s, captioned; numbered
    threads (~3x engagement); daily build-in-public progress. Reply within ~30 min
    (algo halves boost ~every 6h). Documented: 0 -> 2,400 followers in 4 mo -> $8k
    MRR day one. [V] Effort: high, compounding.

  Hacker News (Show HN)  [BEST single-day spike + best dev-tool conversion]
    "Show HN: a local-first node OS you can run and inspect." Plain title, no hype,
    genuinely runnable (ocraft being local-first + MIT is exactly the try-able
    framing HN rewards). Front page ~35-50 uniques/min; dev tools convert 2-5% (up
    to 10.9%). Don't fire from zero — build the X audience first. [V]

  YouTube  [slow trust/SEO MOAT, not front line]
    Story-driven devlogs / "I built X". ~1 sub per 100-500 views; 1k->10k subs takes
    1-3 yrs. The blog versions ("how I built the script-node engine") drive long-tail
    SEO + GitHub stars for years. This is the existing youtube-content-plan's home. [E]

  Reddit  [r/SideProject primary; niche subs secondary]
    Post the building STORY, not an ad; 9:1 rule; needs account age/karma first.
    Range 200 -> 50k uniques. [V]

  Shorts / TikTok / Reels  [awareness layer — repurpose the SAME clip, ~0 marginal cost]
    Highest raw reach, lowest intent. YouTube Shorts prioritized for dev/B2B. [E]

  Devlog / blog + RSS  [compounds for years]
    Own domain first, cross-post to dev.to/Hashnode with canonical link. Migration
    guides + "how I built X" deep-dives are the long-tail SEO engine. [V]

  Telegram  [funnel, not discovery]
    A build-in-public log channel; high-intent once opted in, no organic reach. [V]


5. MONETIZATION INSTRUMENTS — RANKED FOR A TINY-AUDIENCE SOLO DEV
----------------------------------------------------------------

(best fit / lowest audience first; mapped to ocraft + the UA-rail constraint)

  1. DIGITAL PRODUCTS (scene/template/asset/sprite packs, "node starter kits")
     Smallest audience needed (~880 engaged subs sold $6k in 4h for Vassallo [V]).
     Sell via Polar/Lemon Squeezy/Gumroad (MoR handles VAT) — but those need a
     foreign entity for UA payout, so until incorporated, route one-off packs
     through whatever the Patreon/Open Collective rail can pay, or verify Gumroad's
     UA payout separately. THIS IS THE STARTING PRODUCT.

  2. PAID TEMPLATES/PACKS built ON the free tool (Tailwind UI pattern) — overlaps
     with #1; the framing is "premium content made with ocraft." Pairs with
     generative-art / pixel-art-svg / binary-node roadmap items.

  3. CONSULTING / "hire me" — tiny audience (3-6 clients), low-mid 5 figures each;
     MIT adopters are pre-qualified leads (Filippo Valsorda ~Google comp from 6
     retainer clients). Doesn't scale, but real cash early. [V]

  4. RECURRING SUPPORT — Patreon (UA-viable via Payoneer) as the day-one button +
     Open Collective for project funding. Expect ~$0 until audience grows; turn it
     on anyway (zero cost, compounds). NOTE: this REPLACES "GitHub Sponsors" in the
     ranking because GitHub Sponsors itself is closed to UA residents. [V]

  5. SPONSORWARE (features unlock at goals) — strong SECOND-STAGE tactic once a few
     thousand engaged followers exist (Caleb Porzio coined it: 23->75 sponsors,
     $573->$1,560/mo in 2 days — but he already had ~10k X + 3.4k email). ocraft's
     typed-node model makes goals natural ("at 50 supporters I ship the physics
     node"). Doubles as build-in-public content. Not pre-audience. [V]

  6. COURSES / paid content — high upfront (months), big upside (Josh Comeau $550k
     pre-launch on a ~20k list). The tool + free docs are the funnel. Later. [V]

  7. OPEN-CORE HOSTED SaaS — PARKED (see section 2.4): highest effort, multi-year
     ops, most clonable. Revenue would be the HOSTING, not donations. [V]


6. PHASED IMPLEMENTATION PLAN  (sequenced, PR-able / actionable steps)
---------------------------------------------------------------------

PHASE 0 — FOUNDATIONS (week 0-1; do before any "donate button")
  [ ] DECISION: pick rail strategy 1(a) individual or 1(b) incorporate. Default:
      start 1(a), revisit 1(b) when a real product exists.
  [ ] Set up Payoneer; create a Patreon (payout USD/EUR, file W-8BEN).
  [ ] Confirm Open Source Collective pays UA via Wise (email them); if yes, move
      the repo under a GitHub ORG and apply.
  [ ] Add .github/FUNDING.yml pointing at the UA-VIABLE rails only (open_collective:
      + a custom: Patreon URL). NOT a bare github: button.
  [ ] One reachable public support goal with a stated REASON ("at $X/mo I go
      part-time on ocraft"); descriptive tiers, one anchor tier ~$10-15 + a one-time
      "coffee" tier + a high "logo/priority" business tier. Structure once — tiers
      are painful to restructure later.
  [ ] README: tighten the top-of-fold pitch + add a 15-30s demo GIF/clip (the
      single highest-converting README asset for a visual tool).

PHASE 1 — AUDIENCE (week 1-12; the load-bearing phase, ~$0 revenue expected)
  [ ] Stand up the channels: X account, a devlog (own domain), a Telegram log
      channel, a YouTube channel (devlogs). Cross-link.
  [ ] Cadence: 5-7 X posts/week, each anchored on ONE demo clip; one weekly recap
      thread ("shipped X, metric Y, next Z"). Reply daily to 20-30 niche accounts.
  [ ] Produce one "signature" original piece (a standout scene / generative-art
      demo / "a note that IS a program" clip) to prove value before reply-guying
      converts.
  [ ] Make ONE Screen-Studio clip per feature; repurpose each to Shorts/TikTok/
      Reels and a devlog post. Build the clip library that becomes the launch reel.
  [ ] Start an EMAIL LIST early (the actual asset). Lead magnet = a free starter
      pack (a teaser of the future paid packs).
  [ ] Write 2-4 SEO devlog deep-dives ("how I built the script-node engine / the
      node OS / the scene renderer") — the long-tail compounding engine.
  GATE to Phase 2: ~1,000-3,000 engaged followers AND a few hundred email subs.

PHASE 2 — LAUNCH (when Phase-1 gate is met)
  [ ] Polish ocraft into a genuinely runnable Show HN: one-command run, a hosted
      read-only demo or a great clip, crisp "what it is in one line."
  [ ] "Show HN: a local-first node OS you can run and inspect." US-morning ET, then
      reply for hours. Same day / week: Product Hunt + r/SideProject (building story).
  [ ] Convert the launch spike into email subs (not just stars) — the stars don't
      convert later; the list does.

PHASE 3 — FIRST REVENUE (digital product on the free core)
  [ ] Ship product #1: a premium pack built ON ocraft (scene2d/3d template pack OR
      generative-art recipe pack OR pixel-art SVG sprite set). Validate with a
      Stripe-link / pre-order BEFORE building the full pack.
  [ ] Route the sale through a UA-viable rail; if volume justifies it, trigger the
      1(b) incorporation decision to unlock Polar/Gumroad/Stripe (MoR VAT).
  [ ] First ~10 customers will be manual/1:1 (DMs, email, niche-community replies),
      not the launch — plan for that.

PHASE 4 — SCALE THE WORKING LEVER (only after one channel/product clearly works)
  [ ] Double down on whichever converted (more packs, a commercial license page, a
      sponsorware goal, or consulting inbound).
  [ ] Consider a course (high upfront) once the list is ~10k.
  [ ] Re-evaluate open-core SaaS ONLY if there is real pull AND the keystone +
      sandbox are shipped (multi-tenant prerequisite). Gate paid modules AGPL/
      source-available, not MIT.


7. METRICS & DECISION GATES
---------------------------
  - Audience: weekly follower delta, email subs (the real number), clip view->profile
    and profile->follow rates. Gate to launch: ~1-3k engaged followers + a few
    hundred subs.
  - Funnel: follower->email (target 2-5%), email->sale (target 2-5%).
  - Revenue: treat recurring (Patreon/OC) as ~$0 baseline; judge the BUSINESS on
    digital-product sales + consulting, not donations.
  - Incorporation trigger: pull Phase-3 forward only when product demand is proven
    (pre-orders), since 1(b) has real overhead.


8. RISKS & HONEST CAVEATS
-------------------------
  - UA-payout is the #1 risk. Confirm OSC-via-Wise and Patreon-via-Payoneer with the
    platforms + a UA accountant before building on either. [E on OSC-UA specifically]
  - No audience = no revenue. Median Sponsors income ~$0; the plan's first 3 months
    are deliberately revenue-free audience work. Budget for that emotionally.
  - The Dendron trap: don't take VC for a free self-hostable tool; if you ever step
    back, HAND OFF maintainership rather than orphaning it.
  - MIT is the most clonable license — fine for the core (it's the funnel), but gate
    any future paid hosted modules under AGPL/source-available.
  - Obsidian's ~$25M ARR / $350M valuation figures are widely reported but NOT
    confirmed by Obsidian (one tracker says ~$2M) — directional, not gospel. [E]
  - Time cost: the audience phase is the expensive part (high, compounding effort).
    It competes with build time. The reconciliation is that build-in-public makes
    the building itself the content.
