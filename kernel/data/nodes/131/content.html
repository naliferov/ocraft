1. Ancient Philosophy

  • Pre-Socratic philosophy (7th-5th centuries BC): Thales, Anaximander, Heraclitus, Pythagoras.
  • Classical philosophy (5th-4th centuries BC): Socrates, Plato, Aristotle.
  • Hellenistic philosophy (4th-1st centuries BC): Stoicism, Epicureanism, Skepticism.

2. Medieval Philosophy

  • Early medieval philosophy (5th-10th centuries): Augustine of Hippo, Boethius.
  • Scholasticism (11th-14th centuries): Anselm of Canterbury, Thomas Aquinas, Duns Scotus.

3. Early Modern Philosophy

  • Renaissance (15th-16th centuries): Pico della Mirandola, Nicholas of Cusa.
  • Rationalism (17th century): René Descartes, Baruch Spinoza, Gottfried Leibniz.
  • Empiricism (17th-18th centuries): Francis Bacon, John Locke, David Hume.
  • Enlightenment (18th century): Immanuel Kant, Voltaire, Jean-Jacques Rousseau.

4. Modern Philosophy

  • German idealism (late 18th - early 19th centuries): J. G. Fichte, F. W. J. Schelling, G. W. F. Hegel.
  • Existentialism (19th-20th centuries): Søren Kierkegaard, Friedrich Nietzsche, Jean-Paul Sartre.
  • Pragmatism (late 19th - early 20th centuries): Charles Peirce, William James, John Dewey.
  • Phenomenology (20th century): Edmund Husserl, Martin Heidegger, Maurice Merleau-Ponty.
  • Structuralism and post-structuralism (20th century): Claude Lévi-Strauss, Jacques Derrida, Michel Foucault.

5. Postmodernism and Contemporary Philosophy

  • Postmodernism (second half of the 20th century): Jean-François Lyotard, Jean Baudrillard.
  • Contemporary currents (21st century): Postcolonial philosophy, Feminist philosophy, Philosophy of science and technology.
