proposition - the meaning of an affirmative sentence

pushes mathematics into the background and studies the purely formal properties of symbols represented as strings

Propositional logic studies the properties of propositions in terms of their constants and references to variables, such as A, B, C, ... and five operators: AND, OR, IMPLIES, EQUALS, and NOT. As well as using the symbols ∧, ∨, →, ↔, ¬.

The corresponding logical operations are conjunction, disjunction, implication, equivalence, and negation. All of these operations, except NOT, are binary. The meaning of these operations is described by truth value tables.
