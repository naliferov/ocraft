durov: ran large social platforms ~20y; seen every govt tactic to suppress freedoms (pr, legal, political, manipulation, official excuses). authoritarian tricks (russia/china/iran) now surface in the west.

trick 1 — surveillance under a "child protection" pretext. UK: thousands arrested/yr over social-media posts (politically incorrect → fined/prison). germany: arrested for insulting politicians online — call one a pig/thief → up to 3y prison, thousands/yr; people stop using real names. EC: a law requiring ID to access social media — pretext "protect children," real aim = control political speech (same justification authoritarian regimes used for a century; bypasses debate/rationality). "chat control": for 5y the EC has pushed to force every messaging app to backdoor its encryption. franklin: those who trade essential liberty for temporary safety deserve neither liberty nor safety. ask russians/iranians if they're safer than 10-15y ago — lost freedoms, no safety gained. it's ordinary people who lose privacy, not criminals.

trick 2 — data over-collection → theft & abuse. durov ran VK (largest russian social network) 15y ago: russian police sent data requests; VK disclosed private-message contents in some cases (unlike telegram, which never does). targets had nothing to do with the crimes cited → he "investigated the investigators": police were corrupt, accessing innocents' messages to blackmail them and sell data to criminals + harassment. the west isn't safe either: last year a french tax official was caught stealing the financial data of crypto/wealth holders and selling it to criminals. that + other french-govt leaks → kidnappings surged: >40 victims in france in the first 3 months of 2026 (tortured, harassed, humiliated) — govt wanted too much data and failed to protect it. and they want more: access to everyone's private messages.

trick 3 — selective enforcement. authoritarian tactic now exported east→west: pass a flood of excessive, contradictory laws → compliance impossible → every entrepreneur is technically a criminal → govt picks whom to prosecute. comply / give political favors → left alone; defend citizens' rights / refuse → full persecution, excused as "we're just enforcing the law; you don't want a lawless country." durov: a criminal case against him in 2013, and one in russia today. elon musk: the EC offered a secret deal — censor certain political narratives in exchange for waived fines/laws; musk refused, most platforms accepted, musk got a €120m EC fine. durov himself: arrested in paris 2024 (reasons still unclear), offered a secret deal by the head of french intelligence to silence political voices; detained ~3 days in a cell. his court-appointed translator — a woman who'd escaped the USSR — said she felt the soviet union catching up with her, "back in the USSR."

the new danger is ai. USSR, late 80s: parents' letters/telegrams were safe — the KGB couldn't read everything (finite officers, finite hours). ai erases that human limit: every message monitored & analyzed, every draft/thought/idea scored, every relationship mapped → a future darker than the USSR of the 80s.

unlike the titanic's passengers, there's nowhere to escape — dissidents used to flee to the west; soon they won't be able to tell if they've left a repressive homeland or entered another open-air prison. no backup plan, no lifeboats, no second west, no backup civilization. the west has been the guiding star for everyone who loves freedom; if western freedom sinks, the rest follows. fix the ship.
