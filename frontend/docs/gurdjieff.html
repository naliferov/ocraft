Non-identification

To learn, as far as possible, not to identify with surrounding conditions — while continuing to observe inner and outer manifestations even when subject to the most varied biased feelings.

The goal: to become convinced with one's whole being of one's negative qualities — not only with the mind, but wholly — in order to become once again a person who wishes to work on oneself with one's whole being, and not with consciousness alone.

Threefold attention

The fourth preparatory exercise: to observe simultaneously three heterogeneous results from different sources:

  • registering the process of "sensation" (one finger)
  • registering the process of "feeling" (another finger)
  • counting automatic movements (a third finger)

Key formula

"If 'I am', only then 'I can'; if 'I can', only then do I deserve and have the objective right to want. Without the capacity to 'be able', there exists no possibility of having anything; nor any right to it."
