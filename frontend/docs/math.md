## Geometry

-   line — has neither a beginning nor an end
-   ray — has a beginning, no end
-   segment — part of a line, bounded by two points

Regular (equilateral) triangle: all sides equal, all angles = 60°. The Pythagorean theorem (the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares of the legs) and trigonometric functions apply only to right triangles. For an arbitrary triangle, use the law of sines or the law of cosines.
