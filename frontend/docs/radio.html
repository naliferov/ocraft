Lower frequency → longer wavelength AM — amplitude modulation (MF band) FM — frequency modulation (VHF band)

HF (shortwave) — High Frequency

  • Frequency: 3–30 MHz
  • Who uses it: radio amateurs, the military, maritime communications
  • Characteristic: reflects off the ionosphere → long-range communication
  • Line of sight: not required

VHF — Very High Frequency

  • Frequency: 30–300 MHz
  • Who uses it: radio amateurs, aviation, public services, walkie-talkies
  • Characteristic: barely reflects → line-of-sight
  • Line of sight: desirable

UHF — Ultra High Frequency

  • Frequency: 300–3000 MHz
  • Who uses it: walkie-talkies, mobile communications, Wi-Fi, drones
  • Characteristic: does not reflect, poorly diffracts around obstacles
  • Line of sight: required

SHF — Super High Frequency

  • Frequency: 3–30 GHz
  • Who uses it: satellites, radar, microwave relay links, 5G
  • Characteristic: almost a "laser," affected by weather
  • Line of sight: mandatory
