Parts of the psyche

The Child — reacts quickly, spontaneously, unconsciously. Panic = no action. It is closed off in three steps: contact (attention to the bodily sensation of the emotion) → warmth and tenderness (the tension is part of me, there is no need to remove it) → safety (a warm feeling toward the tension).

The Adolescent — seeks recognition, uniqueness, acceptance; competes to be special → pride, rationalizes ("everything is for the best"). Themes: relationships, projects, hanging out with interesting people. It perceives loneliness as a deficiency. Everyone has individuality and it requires no effort — only relaxation and giving up the race for external recognition. When it doesn't get the approval it expected, an unpleasant wave rises — the adult places attention and understanding there, thereby calming the adolescent and winning its trust.

The Adult — is aware of states without dissolving into them; is larger than any situation, keeps its footing and composure. It is in no hurry, gives the child as much attention as needed (rushing to move on is the adolescent). It resolves the issues of the child and adolescent without compensations. The child merges only with the adult: with constant attention and warmth the child's tension relaxes, and it ceases to feel separate. Contact with the child is only through sensations, not images; any state must be accepted as one's own → wholeness.

The body signals states all day long — you need to move toward them, not turn away; the body is a companion for life. Distinguish which part is active right now, and track reactions through a wave journal .

Values of the adult

  1. Mastery — becoming strong in your craft
  2. Autonomy — running your own life
  3. Understanding — seeing reality clearly
  4. Creation — making something valuable (including for others)
  5. Depth of experience — living richly

Reactions to confusion/splitting

The Child : passively — suppression, tears, withdrawal into oneself; actively — aggression, shouting, trying to "force through" the situation.
The Adolescent : passively — "this doesn't concern me"; actively — a display of decisiveness, strength, independence.

Thank the adolescent for trying to be the adult (but its psyche isn't meant for that), relieve it of responsibility, and become a significant figure for it.

Questions and answers

  • The parts switch on in turn: child (warmth, safety) → adolescent (recognition) → adult (decisions).
  • Depression is more often connected with unsatisfied adolescent pride than with childhood sadness (which is easy to switch off).
  • There is no need to blame yourself — the task is to notice states and support yourself with care and acceptance.
  • The child lives in a virtual world, the adolescent in a pseudo-reality, the adult in reality; all three worlds exist simultaneously.

Subpersonalities: the inner child, adolescent, adult; the parent, the sage, the teacher.
