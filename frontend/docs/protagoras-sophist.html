Protagoras wrote a work that became the cause of his death — the treatise "On the Gods." From it the following phrase has come down to us: "Concerning the gods, I cannot know either that they exist, or that they do not exist, or what they are like in form. For many things prevent us from knowing this: both the obscurity of the question and the brevity of human life."

"On every matter there are two opinions opposed to each other."

"Since man lives in a particular 'linguistic reality' that does not reflect actuality faithfully, truth is formed in the space of human communication."

My thought latched onto this. Reflecting reality is, one way or another, a reflection of one's own reality. This lets you sense that automatic contraband that has been smuggled into us and set off at the necessary moment. Contraband about how to reflect and carry something across in such a way that others, too, can reflect it. This utilitarianism hums within us, feeding the skill of survival, and accordingly, once that task of survival is accomplished, it drifts on in the form of enslaving other movements by this movement — or of reflection... One reflection for the sake of another, one for the other, being for the other.
