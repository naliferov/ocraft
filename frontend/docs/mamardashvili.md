Do not resolve an experience through reactive action — remain suspended within it. A reactive resolution (revenge) cancels out the very experience itself. Productive suffering is not resolved by anything

The labor of suffering means holding yourself at the point of non-action, without trying to resolve or substitute. By analogy with Freud's "labor of mourning."

When one turns the other cheek, this is an ontological truth: a returning slap destroys the very thing one was meant to labor over.

* * *

### The point of equilibrium

The point at which "the weights of the whole earth have converged" — maximally saturated and at the same time empty. In it everyone is leveled — the prince and the pauper. From any point, from any filth or splendor, one can rise. It is also called the point of the **Unknown, or the Other** .

* * *

### The courage of the impossible / Heroism

Whatever happens, regardless of time and place — I can. This presupposes the acceptance of a fundamental solitude.

> "Only that person is free who is ready and has the real strength for the labor of freedom"

Not hope (revolutionaries hope), but the continuous reproduction of the foundation within oneself. The "machine of the eternity of desire."

A heroic view of the world — a world in which there are no intentions toward us.

* * *

### Experimental faith

Reality as a hidden continuous process can be the object only of such a faith as allows one to engage with it without guarantees given in advance, at one's own peril and risk.

> "The impossible is not that which is possible without me — the impossible is that which holds by my faith, and only by faith in it"

"Experimental" — because in present, genuine movement, experiment reveals the actual face of reality.

* * *

### The work (opera operans)

The distinction between **opera operata** (the produced work) and **opera operans** (the producing work). An analog of the distinction natura naturata / natura naturans.

The work is not a finished object but a continuously being-born reality. It cannot be considered acquired once and for all. It is also called the **Last Judgment** — that in the face of which "we are not free."

The work is a self-legislating machine (τέχνος) that produces only correct harmonic combinations. Without the work there are no structures, only a reactive entanglement in causes and effects.

* * *

### The mystery of time / The eternal present

Not the instant through which the past rolls over into the future — but a voluminous, dynamic region. Our momentary demands fragment this continuity, demanding immediate realization.

The ability to hold time — to keep oneself within the "mystery of time," without trying to resolve the unfinished.

> "It is always already too late" — every minute is a minute of the end of history. There is always something that must be brought to an end rather than dragged along undone.

* * *

### Involuntary memory (Proust)

If experience is held back, suspended in "amechania" — one can pass into the rupture created by the dislocation. Involuntary recollections help where voluntary effort does not reach.

A state "between past and present" — in it happiness without hope is possible.

* * *

### Consciousness vs Knowledge

Consciousness is not knowledge. What remains on the side of "the feeling of being alive" — that is consciousness.

Consciousness is the consciousness of **a change of inclination** — sensation is an inclination (we incline toward something), and consciousness is the awareness of the change of this inclination.

Logical operations (observation, inference) coexist quite well with the psychological mechanisms of fear, hope — and do not lead to reality. Thinking is possible even without an exit from unreality.

* * *

### Ontological experience

Something that does not arise out of experience but must either be or not be. When it is — experience receives meaning. The coinciding "the same about the same" — not the prolongation of the old, but the newly born.

> "The world does not live by rules and laws, although, if it lives, rules and laws are realized in it"

* * *

### The law of the inexpressible

Conversation cannot transmit from one person to another that inexpressible thing which distinguishes precisely his experience. Everything said is "the wreckage of the shipwreck of what we wanted to say."

Art can show — through equivalents — that which cannot be conveyed directly in words.

* * *

### The telescope

To see the large where others see a trifle. To toss in bed and be unable to sleep is a trifle, but one can describe it only by looking through a telescope: you see an enormous star where there seems to be a tiny little circle.

* * *

### The possible human

A vertical cross-section over and across all cultures. The person (not the individual) — something that is the foundation of itself.

> "I act — not because such is the law, not because there are empirical causes, but I act — because I act"

Our desires arise where some possible countenance wants to come into being. To desire means to desire the other. The cause of desire is more important than the object of desire.

**The possible human as emptiness** — "The person is precisely emptiness, and if there is already something, then it is no longer the person but something classifiable." A tensed emptiness, which is the more effective the more indeterminate it is. This is the utopos.

* * *

### The utopos / The unknown homeland

Real life — life "in truth" — exists within continuous writing (the text of consciousness). Proust called it "true life." The opposite of chaos and the multiplication of inert things.

> "The artist is a citizen of an unknown country"

Chaadaev called this "the philosophy of the gravedigger": what separates us from that life is not the gravedigger's spade — that life is present in every day, and the task is to destroy the inauthentic life within this one.

* * *

### Continuous writing / The text of consciousness

The topological structure of the contentfulness of consciousness — a movement within which real events and states are induced. Not literal writing, but writing as a mode of existence.

It opposes chaos: when one thing coincides with its image — the multiplication of inert things sets in. Continuous writing is that which holds reality together.

* * *

### Enthusiasm / Lava

The state in which it is worth writing — "a complete renewal of the head, where all the partitions have collapsed, there is no rigidity whatsoever, and our whole substance seems like lava ready to take any form."

The step preceding understanding is the magmatic dissolution of one's own empirical "I," which is always rigid and full of barriers.

Ideas written with enthusiasm give birth to other ideas — thousands of them. Victor Hugo thinks too much instead of letting thinking happen.

* * *

### Understanding the other (Averroes)

Averroes saw a theatrical performance but had no concept of "theater" — and therefore a whole chunk of the world could not enter into him. That which we cannot understand **excludes our life** — because our life consists in solving the problem of accommodating our feelings within the world.

The understanding element (the concept) puts all the other elements in their place — but it itself is not derivable from them and is not visible within them. "Nothing in the field of the eye tells us that it is the eye that sees this."

For something to enter from another person — contact in the space of understanding is needed. To understand the other is like understanding a foreigner, a Martian.

* * *

### Cosa mentale (mind-thing)

A peculiar attachment to us — both mental and material at once. We see by means of it, but we do not see it itself. It is connected with a sensitivity not specified by the sense organs (not hearing, not sight — but feeling).

We hear music, not the vibration of a string. Cosa mentale is that by means of which we perceive.

* * *

### Crystallization

First something happens (an event), and it deposits its causes in the past. The causal chain crystallizes behind the event.

> "The causes of Werther's suicide appeared in the world really — after "Werther" was written"

Now I cannot be tender without Albertine — Descartes: "now it can no longer be."

* * *

### The lightning of impression / The Pythagorean instant

Truth is given only by a single instant — it shakes us instantaneously and just as instantaneously departs, taking pieces of us away into some distance. The minimality of the maximum.

> "Work while you have the light" (John, quoted by Proust)

Truth is spread out over a multitude of points (a cloud of points), but reveals itself only by lightning. Two dimensions of truth: the spatial (many points) and the temporal (a single instant).

* * *

### A form for experience

Forms exist not out of experience but **for experience** — so that for the first time something can be experienced. Kant: "physics is the cognition of the world not by experience but for experience." Leonardo: "nature is full of infinite principles that have never been in experience."

There are ideas that have never been in experience — but by means of which a different experience becomes possible. Fourier: "forms of society that remained to be invented."

* * *

### Time regained

Eternity is not after this life, but a **vertical section** in relation to the horizontal of our life. What is happening now — that is eternal life, if you are alive.

> "Imagine that the whole Universe is a single sentence being spoken, but never said to the end"

Lost time — the twistings and repetitions from which nothing has been extracted into structure. Repentance without the extraction of meaning is psychologically real, philosophically unreal: you will commit the same act again.

* * *

### Descartes (on the primordial gaze)

Descartes lived among the Dutch without knowing the language — in order to regard them as a natural landscape. He wanted to have a **primordial gaze** and to have all the sources of his states only within himself, and not in what language would spontaneously generate.

> "Had he known the language, that language would spontaneously generate in him some meanings and senses"

* * *

### On happiness and the sources of evil (Conversations on Thinking)

We want to build a happy state that would mechanically produce happiness. But the machine will still not deliver happiness — it will deliver only what is called happiness on the radio.

> "We always seek the sources of evil, misfortune, and unhappiness in others. Such a vision of others I call a vision of nonexistences"
