"The world picture, when understood essentially, does not mean a picture depicting the world, but the world grasped as picture"

It is not that the world picture changes from a medieval one into a modern one — rather, the world itself becomes a picture at all. This marks the essence of the modern age.

Greek man — listens to beings. Beings disclose themselves and seize man. Man is not an observer — he abides amid beings, which gaze upon him. The world cannot become a picture within Hellenism.

Modern man — represents. Representation is no longer a listening perception, but a grasping and comprehending. Not the power of what is present, but the dominion of the grip. Representation is coagitatio: a self-grounding intrusion, an objectifying labor.

"Consciousness is the co-represented-ness of the objective sphere together with the representing man within the circle of the representation he himself secures"

Man becomes the subject — he struggles for a position as that being which sets the measure for everything. From this come the struggle of worldviews, the unlimited might of calculation, planning, and organization.

Gigantism — a symptom of the epoch. It breaks through in the form of its own disappearance: the abolition of distances, radio, planning. When gigantism turns from quantity into quality, it becomes impervious to calculation. The incalculable looms as a shadow.

"The shadow is a manifest, though impenetrable, testimony to the hidden luminescence"

The Nothing — is not an empty nullity. The Nothing is never null; it is being itself, to whose truth man will entrust himself once he overcomes himself as subject.

The open Between — there-being (Dasein): the ecstatic region where being steps forth from concealment and withdraws into it.

Descartes — he can be overcome only through overcoming the metaphysics of the modern age as a whole, through posing the question of the meaning of being.

System — when the world becomes a picture, the system comes to power. But without the originary energy of the project, it degenerates into empty formalism. The greatness of the systematics of Leibniz, Kant, Fichte, Hegel, and Schelling lies in this: that it unfolds not from the subject as ego.

"Value is an objectified goal, expressing the needs of the representation that establishes itself in the world as picture"

The scholar disappears — he is replaced by the researcher, enlisted in the staff of an enterprise. He needs no home library. Publishers determine which books are to be written.
