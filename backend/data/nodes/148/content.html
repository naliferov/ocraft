More ready-made:

TurtleBot 4 (Create 3 + Jetson, full ROS 2 navigation stack, ~$1-2k) as a base + mount an arm on it.
Unitree Go2 (quadruped, ~$1.6k) + arm — if you want to climb over thresholds/uneven terrain where wheels get stuck. More expensive and harder to power/balance when the arm moves.
DJI RoboMaster EP — already ships with an optional gripper arm and an official Python SDK, the least hassle.

Least friction overall: take a look at Hello Robot Stretch — it's a commercial mobile manipulator made specifically for research/cleaning (a telescoping arm on a mobile base, Python/ROS API), but this is already the $20k+ tier, not DIY.

Honest difficulties that actually bite (not just on paper):
Perception — the main unsolved piece. "Driving there" (SLAM, navigation) is mature. "Reliably grasping an arbitrary object" is still the frontier. Pick-and-place of known objects in known positions works; "clean up any mess" doesn't. Scope it down to specific objects.
Grasping. Cheap parallel grippers handle a limited class of shapes. This is often a bigger bottleneck than the arm itself.
Powering a mobile arm. Arm servos + base motors on one battery — budget your current draw, otherwise the base will die in 20 minutes.
Stability with the arm extended. The arm's reach shifts the center of mass; on a light base this can tip it over. Either a heavier base, or limit the reach in software.
