Pitch

A lonely young man tries to escape his isolation by pulling women into emotionally dangerous games of silence, tension, and intimacy.

then a person appears who is able to withstand his mode of contact, and that is exactly why the bond becomes "metaphysical."

protagonist, him

parents divorced. he lives alone, and his parents are in different countries

aggressive and reserved

he wants to become the subject of his own life, not the object of circumstances, loneliness, the body, and decay

inside there is feeling, desire, tenderness, sexuality, an impulse toward contact, thought, an image of himself, but between the inner and the outer there is something like a barrier

he doesn't go completely silent, he writes messages, makes contact, looks for girls at social events, reaches out, but feels that he doesn't express himself fully, as if only a weak part of what's inside makes it to the outside

he wants real contact, but he can't withstand the moment when contact becomes real. too much anxiety rises in him. All his demons start ringing.

There is also a more hidden desire for a sudden, direct experience of reality without the inner barrier. that is, for the first time: the world stops feeling separate from him, the constant self-observation disappears, the feeling of "I can't manifest myself" disappears, for the first time he is simply present

broken, but alive. he has humor, a strange dignity, energy, the ability to turn his own decay into performance. your hero can then crack jokes at the wrong moment, play strange games, aestheticize pain, turn loneliness into mythology.

protagonist, her

she: a mirror, a catalyst, a person next to whom his inner world begins to tremble, to open up

the only one who feels his intensity, or, on the contrary, fears it then she becomes a central part of the theme
